Ambiguity

In our research, eight metrics represented the policies regarding key information. To classify policies based on these metrics, we used a supervised learning algorithm known as the random forest classifier. Supervised learning is a reliable approach for classification tasks but requires a significant portion of the dataset to be labeled. Therefore, we partially annotated 100 policies in our dataset, which was sufficient for the random forest classifier to work well for our dataset.

Our research concentrated on how ambiguity affects the effectiveness of classification methods.We found that these algorithms were less accurate for highly nebulous programs, as they have less well-defined conditions and guidelines. Our results highlighted the importance of policy nebulosity as a factor affecting classification models' performance, suggesting further research is needed to improve algorithms that can handle nebulous programs.
