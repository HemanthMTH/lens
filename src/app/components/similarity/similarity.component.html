Comparing Privacy Policies

Calculating the resemblance between a pair of textual documents is a frequent undertaking in natural language processing, featuring numerous real-world uses (e.g., order search engine outcomes or suggest related material to users). By comparing privacy policies we can explore which devices have similar privacy policies, or outlier devices with privacy policies that are significantly different from others of the same type.

Heat Map shown below is a graphical representation of data that uses colors to visualize values of a matrix. In the context of policy text analysis of several manufacturers, a heatmap can be used to represent the similarities in their policies. The matrix would contain pairwise similarity scores between the policies of each manufacturer. By visualizing this matrix as a heatmap, we can quickly identify patterns and similarities between the policies of different manufacturers. The color scheme used in the heatmap can be customized to highlight areas of high and low similarity. Overall, the heatmap provides an easy-to-understand and visually appealing way to analyze and compare the policy texts of different manufacturers.

The following chart presents a Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection (UMAP) of text embeddings, specifically derived from the privacy policy text of various websites. The purpose of this visual representation is to provide a high-level understanding of how similar or dissimilar these policies are to each other, based on their content.

Every point on this chart represents a unique website's privacy policy. The closeness of these points to one another indicates similarity in their privacy policies. In contrast, points that are further apart have more dissimilar policy text, suggesting a divergence in their data privacy practices.
